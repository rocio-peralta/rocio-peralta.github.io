Elegantly solved a problem


So, last week I got stuck on this media query exercise where we had to select a class and return its length. I didn't know what to do, but I didn't suffer in silence. Instead, I went out of my comfort zone and asked a facilitator for help.


Throughout the process, I wasn't exactly frustrated, but the problem was really bothering me. I couldn't stop thinking about it. I realized that obsessively thinking about the problem wasn't helping me. So, I left it alone, put my mind on something else, and then came back to it later with a fresh, clear mind. That helped me a lot more. Even though obsessive thinking can feel satisfying, it rarely helps me solve the problem.


What I learned from this experience is that I don't always have to build everything from scratch with a loop. There's probably a method that already does what I want to do. So, now I try to combine methods or find one that does what I need.


Problem-solving techniques/processes


During this sprint, I feel a lot more confident in my problem-solving skills. There are several techniques that have really helped me out. For example, learning how to read error messages has been a game-changer. When there's a problem, the console guides me in the right direction. Instead of rewriting all the code, I can just focus on the line that's causing the issue.


Trying something is another technique that's been really useful. Sometimes, I'll just make a small change like swapping the order of lines or adding a "." and it actually works! It might be by luck, but then I can focus on understanding why it works that way.


Googling is another key technique, but it's important to read the results carefully. I've found that other people often have the exact same problem as me and have already posted solutions. And when I'm really stuck, asking peers or coaches for help has been a lifesaver.


Finally, reflecting on my problem-solving process has helped me improve even further. I can look back at what worked and what didn't, and then adjust my approach for next time.
